# HRV Interpretation Guide

## Readiness Framework

| HRV vs 7-day Baseline | Resting HR | Signal | Recommended Action |
|----------------------|------------|--------|--------------------|
| >10% above baseline | Normal or low | Green — high readiness | Quality session OK (Z4/Z5) |
| Within ±5% | Normal | Amber — maintain | Planned session as written (Z2–Z3) |
| 5–10% below baseline | Elevated | Amber-low | Drop to Z1–Z2 only |
| >10% below baseline | Elevated | Red — suppressed | Full rest or active recovery only |

## Long-Term Trend Interpretation

- **Rising trend (weeks)** → fitness improving, can gradually increase load
- **Stable trend** → current load is appropriate, stay the course
- **Declining >2 weeks** → accumulated fatigue; reduce volume 20–30%, audit sleep
- **High day-to-day variability** → lifestyle stressors masking signal; focus on sleep consistency

## App-Specific Notes

| App | Key Metric | Green | Yellow | Red |
|-----|-----------|-------|--------|-----|
| Whoop | Recovery % | >67% | 34–66% | <33% |
| Garmin/Firstbeat | HRV Status | Balanced | Unbalanced | Low |
| HRV4Training / Elite HRV | rMSSD (ms) | >personal baseline | ±5% | >10% below |
| Apple Watch / Oura | 7-day trend | Rising | Stable | Declining |

## Key Questions to Always Ask
1. How long has this trend been going on?
2. How has sleep been this week?
3. Any illness, travel, alcohol, or high life stress?

A single low-HRV day is rarely overtraining. Look for trends over 5–7 days.
